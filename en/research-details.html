Spintronics & Ultrafast Magneto-Optical Spectroscopy

Time-Resolved Magneto-Optical Imaging (TRMOI) & Spin Wave Tomography

Research Objective

We visualize spin wave propagation processes occurring within magnetic materials with time resolution of 10 femtoseconds and spatial resolution of 1 micrometer, aiming for fundamental understanding of magnetic physics and applications in spintronic device development. Through our originally developed TRMOI technology and spin wave tomography methods, we pioneer new frontiers in magneto-optical spectroscopy.

Research Methods & Technologies

  • Ultrashort Pulse Laser Technology: Ultrafast optical excitation of magnetic materials using femtosecond pulse lasers
  • Magneto-Optical Imaging: Real-time observation of magnetization dynamics using Faraday and Kerr effects
  • Spin Wave Tomography: Phase analysis-based dispersion relation reconstruction technique development
  • High-Speed CCD/CMOS Imaging: Multi-stage time measurement from microseconds to picoseconds
  • Numerical Simulation: Comparative verification with LLG equation-based magnetization dynamics calculations
