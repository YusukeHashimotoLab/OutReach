November 1, 2024

Development of Automated Experimental Equipment

Our laboratory is developing an automated experimental system that combines an affordable robotic arm (approximately 200,000 yen) with electronic pipettes.

The first prototype features a high-throughput mixing system integrated with a conveyor belt for efficient sample processing.

We are currently developing a second prototype that combines an electronic balance with a magnetic stirrer.

As a proof of concept, we successfully demonstrated fully automated mixing of colored water with systematically varying ratios of red and blue.

We plan to apply this system to various chemical experiments in the future. Stay tuned for more updates!

August 4, 2024

Commemorative Symposium for Establishment of Endowed Research Division

We held a commemorative symposium for the establishment of the Endowed Research Division within the Frontier Research Institute for Interdisciplinary Sciences.

The symposium featured presentations on the founding principles of our research division and future research directions, fostering active discussions with numerous participants.

The importance of industry-academia collaboration in creating the new interdisciplinary field of Materials Process Data Science was reaffirmed.

June 22, 2024

Automated Drawing of Tohoku University Logo Using Robot Arm

The Tomai Laboratory is advancing experimental automation using robotic arms and other technologies.

A fourth-year student demonstrated the robot arm's capabilities by programming it to draw the Tohoku University logo. Through improved precision control technology, the system can now accurately draw complex figures.
